Two files per shape, on purpose

The STL is the cutter: slice it, print it, press it into the slab. The SVG is the template: layout, sizing and stencil work when you want the outline without the plastic. Same geometry, two doors in.

Questions

Before you buy

What exactly do I get?

One ZIP with 150+ earring cutter shapes across 6 families — each shape as a printable STL plus an SVG template — 3 bonus PDF guides and a design manifest.

Do I need a 3D printer?

For the cutters, yes — the STL prints on any FDM printer through any slicer, and plain PLA works. The SVG templates are useful without a printer for stencil and layout work.

I'm a beginner — will this work for me?

Printing a cutter is one of the simpler 3D prints: small, flat, fast. Honestly: you still need basic familiarity with your printer and with conditioning clay. Start with a circle, then take on the families.

Can I sell what I make?
